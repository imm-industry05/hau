International Research Publication (BSBA Students)

School of Business and Accountancy

August 26, 2022

Ten (10) groups of Marketing Management students from the School of Business and Accountancy presented their research papers at the 16th Global Conference with the theme “Vax to the Future: Work in Transition. Of the 10 groups, five (5) successfully published their papers in the Journal of Global Business, accredited by the Andrew Gonzales Philippine Citation Index.


Below are the published works with corresponding researchers:



1. Influence of social media strategies on the brand awareness of the customers: The case of Alfresco-style Restaurants in Pampanga during the pandemic.
Members: Ayra Neil Diola, Crisologo Canlas, Irene Shery Domondon, Athina Abbie Anne Mitu, Jennielyn Samson, and Jee Ann Santos




2. The Impact of Logistics Service Quality on Customer Satisfaction leading to Customer Retention: the case of Shopee customers in Pampanga Members
Members: Cunan, Ma. Aliza D. Escobar, Moses John S. Galang, Naddine Q. Martinez, Pamela Joy V. Punzalan, and Julliene Marla A.




3. The Shift to Digital: Kapampangans' Behavioral Intention to Use Online Food Delivery Services During the Covid-19 Pandemic
Members: Aquino, Nicoll Bianca F., Carlos, Alexis Jhermaine Q., Galang, Clariz Lagman, Angelika C. Plasencia, Alisha Lee Raine V., and Ronquillo, Carl Angelo P.




4. An Analysis of The Marketing Mix on The Clothing Line Industry and Purchase Intention of Kapampangan Shoppers Using the Theory of Planned Behavior
Members: Reyes, Quennie Anne B., Banania, Odelyn C., Dalmacio, Nicole Anne Fhaye S., Manalastas, Jeris Louise F., and Tuliao, Jumelah Claire S.




5. Kapampangan Gen Z's Attitude and Behavior towards Purchasing Sustainable Packaging
Members: Badian, Ji-An B. Concepcion, Marilou D. Dimasangal, Ma. Camille Nabung, Charlize Adrienne E. Nunag, Mikaela Coleen R. Salunga, and Denzel Angelo L.


Congratulations, Angelites. We are proud of you!

About Holy Angel University

Holy Angel University has over 16,000 students, making it one of the biggest schools in Central Luzon. Established in 1933 by local philanthropist Juan D. Nepomuceno, it is considered the Philippines' first Catholic school founded by a layman instead of the usual diocese or religious congregation. It is also the first Catholic high school in the country that is co-educational, instead of the usual exclusively for boys or exclusively for girls.
