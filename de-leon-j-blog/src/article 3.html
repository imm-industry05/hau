Dota 2 Battle pass

Sept 13 2022

Articles

2 Comments

The Valorant Champions Tour (VCT) is a global competitive esports tournament series for the video game Valorant organised by Riot Games, the game's developers. The series runs multiple events throughout each season, culminating in Valorant Champions, the top-level event of the tour.

DOTA

Two weeks after The International 2022 (TI11) Battle Pass’s debut, Valve addressed community criticism and stated that casual players make significantly less progress than they did with the Battle Pass for the previous TI. The Dota 2 community’s criticism over the TI11 Battle Pass has finally reached Valve, however the grind required for leveling up the Battle Pass without purchasing more levels has improved rather than the content itself. Following their research, Valve altered the Weekly Quests’ prizes and offered a new way for players to accumulate BP points. Players will accrue BP points with each match they participate in beginning with Week 2 of the current Battle Pass as follows: 200 BP are awarded when a match is won. 100 BP points are awarded for a defeat. winning or losing a Turbo match awards 100/50 BP. The Weekly Quests point system was also changed to include the following: Players will now receive 1000/1000/1000 Battle Points at 2/8/15 stars for non-Bonus weeks instead of 500/1000/1500 Battle Points at 4/9/15 stars. The first Bonus Week Battle Chest is now offered at 2 stars as opposed to 3 stars for Bonus weeks. A free week of Dota Plus has also been made available to any Battle Pass owners who have participated in a Dota 2 match to make up for an issue with the service that affected item suggestions. Despite the fact that the point system has been tweaked, Valve did not provide any information regarding the abundance of material marked as “coming soon” in the TI11 Battle Pass.
