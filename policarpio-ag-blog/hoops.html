Air Jordan 36 “First Light”

The Air Jordan 36 First Light boasts a heavy-duty jacquard Leno-Weave upper reinforced with a TPU ribbon that surrounds the collar.

Jordan Luka 1 “Neo Turquoise”

The Jordan Luka 1 is arriving in a white and metallic silver base and neo turquose and bright crimson in details. The color scheme is a nod to his uniform with Real Madrid.

Jordan Zion 2 “Hope Diamond”

Inspired by the 21-year-old's goal of winning at the next level of his career, the upcoming colorway indulges in a “White,” “Half Blue,” “Deep Royal Blue” and “Pink Prime” mix of colors that make a statement on the court.

Jordan Why Not Zero.5 “HTG Honor the Gift”

Jordan Brand is fusing together both Russell Westbrook's street-fashion label Honor The Gift and his latest signature Jordan Why Not Zer0.5 shoe.

Jordan Zoom Separate

Create separation and get buckets on the hardwood in the Jordan Zoom Separate. Inspired by Luka Doncic’s step-back-shot, these silhouettes feature a lightweight, low-top design that helps you create separation faster and take control of the court.

Jordan One Take 3

Lightweight, and hyper-responsive underfoot the Jordan One Take 3 up the ante. Feet get the most out of the Air Zoom Unit, thanks to its top-loaded design.

Jorda React Elevation "Black Volt"

The Jordan React Elevation is inspired by team players who beat opponents with their grit and tenacity at both ends of the court. Light and durable, it offers a secure, supportive fit. An underfoot combination of responsive cushioning and herringbone traction helps keep you on your game during clutch moments.

Jordan Why Not Zero.2 SE "Fresh Mint"

It features a mixed synthetic material upper with different cuts put together. A translucent midfoot saddle helps increase security of the foot. The outsoles have sections of circular traction patterns that enhance grip. This cored-out pattern offers flexibility as well. The outsole features a curved design to create a smoother heel-to-toe transition.

Jordan Why Not Zero.3

As much as the Jordan Why Not Zero.3 is an all-around performer, you can’t ignore the fact that it’s mainly shifted towards a flashy, agile, and athletic player like Westbrook himself.

Jordan Why Not Zero.4 "Upbinging"

The cushion on the Why Not Zer0. 4 is excellent. The double-stacked Zoom technology in the forefoot was abundantly felt in each step and gave a dominantly cushy but also stable ride when running down the court.

Jordan Jumpman 2021 PF

Inspired by the design of the latest Air Jordan game shoe, the Jordan Jumpman 2021 helps up-and-coming players level up their game. The shoe features responsive Zoom Air cushioning in the forefoot. Curved Flightwire cables are stitched into the material for a close, secure fit for competitive play. This PF version is ideal for use on outdoor courts..

Air Jordan 37 "Hare"

The key inspiration for the 37 sees remnants of Jordan 7 designs surrounding the outsole and tongue. The shoe is constructed with a lightweight material using a translucent leno-weave and exoskeleton for additional support. The midsole also features a double-stacked Zoom bag that is situated at the forefoot as well as a carbon fiber plate at the midfoot and Formula 23 foam for the heel for added comfort and stability.

Jordan Westbrook One Take "Rage Green"

The Jordan Westbrook One Take is a takedown model from Russell Westbrook’s signature line with Jordan Brand. It features a predominantly mesh upper along with nubuck and smooth leather overlays placed all throughout the shoe. Highlights of the design include an aggressive zig zag midsole, leather wings around the laces, synthetic reinforcement on the toe, Westbrook’s logo on the tongue, and Jumpman branding on the ankles.

Jordan Zion 1 "Noah"

With a down-to-earth persona and abilities that are out of this world, Zion is unlike anybody else. On court, the gentle spirit who's all about family transforms into an unmatched force of unstoppable athleticism and speed. The Zion 1 kicks off his signature line with a design inspired by his duality of humility meets superhuman ability. Full-length Air Strobel cushioning is stacked with a Zoom Air unit in the forefoot—a sensational mix of plush underfoot comfort and rapid responsiveness. It's light, stable and strong, with aggressive traction to help Zion grip the court and control his power.

Jordan CP3.XII

Crafted with a performance knit upper and leather quarter on the panel, the model is tied together with a lacing system for stability. Aside from the usual Jordan branding hits on the toe box and heel, Paul is taking this opportunity to debut his newest player logo—the owl. Known as the wisest animal of them all, the emblem consists of his family members’ zodiac symbols and can be found neatly placed on the tongue.

Jordan Zoom Zero Gravity

Featuring responsive forefoot cushioning, the Jordan Zoom Zero Gravity PF is designed to be fast and agile. Its lightweight upper provides support, while a midfoot saddle combines with the laces to help stabilize your foot for a snug fit.
